# Video_Game_Analysis

An exploratory data analysis project examining global video game sales data to uncover trends across platforms, genres, regions, and publishers, with the goal of helping video game publishers make data driven decisions about game development, marketing, and pricing.

---

## Project Description

This project analyses a dataset of video games with sales greater than 100,000 copies to provide insights and recommendations for improving sales and revenue. The findings are intended to help video game publishers make informed decisions about game development, marketing strategy, and pricing.

**Dataset:** Video games sales dataset

**Tools Used:** Excel, Power Query, Tableau

---

## Key Questions

1. What are the top selling video games by platform and genre, and how do sales vary by region?
2. Are there any trends in video game sales over time, and how do they vary by platform and genre?
3. What is the impact of a game's rating on its sales, and how does this vary by genre and region?
4. Which publishers have the highest and lowest sales, and what factors contribute to their success or failure?
5. What are the most popular genres and platforms in different regions, and how can publishers tailor their marketing strategies accordingly?

---

## Findings and Insights

### 1. Top Selling Games by Platform and Genre, and Regional Variation
Action, Sports, Shooter, and Role Playing are the strongest performing genres globally, with North America consistently leading regional sales across nearly every genre.

![Regional Sales vs Genre](Regional%20Sales%20vs%20Genre.png)

By platform, PS2, X360, and PS3 lead in North America and Europe, while Japan shows a distinct preference, favouring DS, PS, PS2, SNES, and NES over the platforms that dominate elsewhere.

![Regional Sales vs Platform](Regional%20Sales%20vs%20Platform.png)

### 2. Sales Trends Over Time
Global video game sales rose steadily from 2001, peaked between 2007 and 2009, then declined consistently through to 2020.

![Sum of Global Sales vs Year](SUM%20of%20Global_Sales%20vs.%20Year.png)
![Year vs Global Sales (Tableau)](Sheet%201.png)

### 3. Impact of Game Rating on Sales
Game rating has a measurable impact on global sales, though the effect varies by region, with some genres proving more rating sensitive than others.

### 4. Publisher Performance
Nintendo holds the highest sales among all publishers. The lowest performing publishers, including Majesco Entertainment, Dramatic Create, Falcom Corporation, Ubisoft, Destineer, Kemco, Infogrames, Activision, 7G//AMES, and Wanadoo, were largely affected by the platforms their games were released on, several of which recorded low sales across all regions regardless of publisher.

![Sum of Global Sales vs Platform](SUM%20of%20Global_Sales%20vs.%20Platform.png)

### 5. Regional Genre and Platform Preferences
Globally, PS2 is the most used platform for video games, with PS2, X360, PS3, Wii, and DS rounding out the top five. Regional preferences diverge significantly: North America's genre preferences mirror the global pattern, with Racing ranking third locally. Japan stands apart with a strong preference for Role Playing games, which account for 27.3% of its sales, followed by Action at 12.4%.

---

## Summary of Findings

- North America accounts for 49.2% of global sales, followed by Europe (27.3%), Japan (14.6%), and other regions (8.9%)
- Platforms including 3DO, GG, NG, PCFX, SCD, TG16, and WS recorded very low or negligible sales across all regions
- Publisher success is closely tied to platform choice rather than publisher identity alone, suggesting underperforming publishers could improve results by releasing on stronger platforms
- Regional genre preference varies meaningfully, with Japan favouring Role Playing games far more than any other region

---

## Recommended Actions

**Game Development Recommendations**
- Prioritise development resources toward platforms and genres proven to perform well in each target region rather than applying a uniform global strategy
- Avoid platforms with consistently low or negligible sales across all regions (3DO, GG, NG, PCFX, SCD, TG16, WS)

**Marketing Recommendations**
- Increase marketing investment in Japan and other underperforming regions where sales remain comparatively low
- Promote Role Playing games specifically in Japan, where the genre is in clear demand
- Continue prioritising Action genre marketing in North America and Europe, where it consistently outperforms other genres
- Use the regional breakdowns of platform, genre, and publisher performance to inform region specific marketing strategies rather than a one size fits all approach
